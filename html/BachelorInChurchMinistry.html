Goals and Objectives

only 60 total units for the span of 1 year. Field Ministry subjects are for reporting purposes only.


Theology 1: Introduction to key doctrines of the Christian faith, focusing on foundational theological concepts.

Church History 1: Study of the early church, major councils, and development of Christian doctrine up to the Reformation.

Book of Acts: In-depth study of the Acts of the Apostles, focusing on the early church and missionary efforts.

Pauline Epistles: Examination of the letters of Paul, their theological significance, and application.

Evangelism & Discipleship: Training in the principles and practices of sharing the gospel and nurturing believers.

Church Planting & Growth: Strategies for planting new churches and fostering church growth.

Homiletics: Introduction to the art and science of preaching, focusing on sermon preparation.

Christian Worldview: Examination of how a biblical worldview shapes thinking and living.

Theology 2: Continuation of systematic theology, covering doctrines like Christology, soteriology, and pneumatology.

Church History 2: Exploration of the Reformation, post-Reformation developments, and modern church history.

Christian Counseling: Principles and practices of counseling from a Christian perspective.

Theology 3: Further exploration of systematic theology, including ecclesiology, eschatology, and sacraments.

Theology 4: Advanced topics in systematic theology, focusing on contemporary theological issues.

Church Government: Study of church polity, especially within the Reformed tradition, and principles of worship.

Foundation of Christian Education: Principles and methods for teaching Christian education.

Introduction to Biblical Theology: Overview of the unifying themes and theological trajectory of the Bible.

Christian Worldview: Examination of how a biblical worldview shapes thinking and living.

World Mission: Study of the biblical basis for mission, history of missions, and contemporary mission strategies.

Leadership Development: Training in leadership principles and their application in ministry contexts.

Christian Ethics: Study of moral theology, including ethical issues from a Christian perspective.
