Master of Arts in Pastoral Studies


GRADUATE PROGRAM



Goals and Objectives

The Degree Master of Arts in Pastoral Studies and Master of Arts in Inter-Cultural Studies curriculum based on Reformed Biblical Faith is structured into a trimester program, offering a summarized theological education in an in-depth, integrated, interactive, and practical approach.

A master's degree program focused on pastoral studies, tailored for individuals with a bachelor’s degree or its equivalent. This program prepares students for roles in pastoral leadership within the Church and/or teaching positions in theological institutions as their chosen vocation. Typically, the program requires two (2) years of academic study to complete.


Biblical Principles of Fulfilled Ministry: This course explores the biblical foundations for a life of fruitful and effective ministry. Emphasis is placed on understanding God's calling, spiritual gifts, and the role of the Holy Spirit in personal and corporate ministry. Students will develop practical strategies for fostering spiritual growth and impactful service within the church and community.

Biblical Tentmaking: Focusing on the Apostle Paul’s model of self-supporting ministry, this course examines the integration of secular work and Gospel proclamation. Students will learn to navigate the challenges and opportunities of bi-vocational ministry while maintaining a strong theological foundation. Practical guidance will be provided for balancing professional work with spiritual leadership.

Social Ethics: This course addresses the application of biblical principles to contemporary social and moral issues. Topics include justice, human dignity, environmental stewardship, and the sanctity of life, approached from a Reformed theological perspective. Students will be equipped to articulate and apply ethical responses rooted in Scripture to societal challenges.

Church Management, Leadership, & Planning: Students will explore the principles of effective church administration, strategic planning, and leadership within the body of Christ. Topics include financial stewardship, human resource management, and conflict resolution, grounded in a biblical framework. The course aims to prepare leaders to build healthy, mission-focused church communities.

Comparative Religion, Cults, and Occults: This course provides a biblical critique of world religions, cults, and occult practices. Students will examine their historical development, core beliefs, and practices, contrasting them with the truths of Scripture. Emphasis is placed on equipping students for apologetics and effective evangelism in a pluralistic society.

Pastoral Theology: Pastoral Theology explores the spiritual, relational, and administrative dimensions of pastoral ministry. Students will study the biblical qualifications, responsibilities, and challenges of pastoral leadership. Practical instruction is provided in preaching, counseling, sacraments, and shepherding the flock.

Christian Thought & Philosophy: This course investigates the interplay between Christian theology and philosophical thought throughout history. Students will examine key thinkers, ideas, and debates that have shaped Christian doctrine and practice. Emphasis is placed on developing a biblically grounded worldview to engage contemporary philosophical issues.

Kingdom, Covenant, and Canon of the Bible: Students will delve into the overarching themes of God’s Kingdom, covenant relationships, and the structure of the biblical canon. The course provides a cohesive understanding of Scripture’s unity and diversity, emphasizing its authority and inspiration. Practical application of these themes in ministry and discipleship is a central focus.

21st Century Leadership & Ministry: This course equips students to address the challenges and opportunities of ministry in the modern age. Topics include technological engagement, cultural shifts, and innovative leadership strategies. Students will develop skills for leading churches and ministries in a rapidly changing world while remaining faithful to biblical principles.

Church Polity & Biblical Worship: Exploring the governance and worship practices of the church, this course emphasizes a biblical and Reformed understanding of church polity. Students will study historical and contemporary models of church government and worship, focusing on their theological foundations. Practical guidance for implementing orderly and God-honoring practices is provided.

Research & Writing (Thesis): This capstone course prepares students to conduct rigorous theological research and produce a scholarly thesis. Emphasis is placed on developing research methodologies, academic writing skills, and critical engagement with theological literature. Students will demonstrate their mastery of Reformed biblical faith and its application to ministry through their final project.
